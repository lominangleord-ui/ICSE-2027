Science (52) – Biology

Science Paper 3  |  ICSE 2027

Code 52
Class X
Written Paper: 80 Marks | Duration: 2 Hours
Internal Assessment (Practical): 20 Marks
1. Basic Biology – Cell Cycle, Chromosome & Genetics ▼

(i) Cell Cycle and Cell Division

  • Cell cycle: Interphase (G1, S, G2) and Mitotic phase
  • Mitosis and its stages
  • Meiosis as a reduction division (stages not required)
  • Homologous chromosomes, crossing over and variations
  • Significance and major differences between mitotic and meiotic division

(ii) Structure of Chromosome

  • Basic structure: chromatin, chromatid, gene structure of DNA, centromere

(iii) Genetics

  • Mendel's three laws of inheritance
  • Monohybrid cross – phenotype and genotype; Dihybrid cross – phenotype only
  • Terms: gene, allele, heterozygous, homozygous, dominant, recessive, mutation, variation, phenotype, genotype
  • Sex determination in human beings
  • Sex-linked inheritance: haemophilia and colour blindness (X-linked only)
2. Plant Physiology (Class X) ▼

(i) Absorption and Osmosis

  • Imbibition, diffusion, osmosis; osmotic pressure, root pressure; turgidity and flaccidity
  • Plasmolysis and deplasmolysis; active and passive transport
  • Rise of water up to the xylem; forces responsible for ascent of sap
  • Structure of a single full-grown root hair; cohesive and adhesive forces; transpirational pull

(ii) Transpiration

  • Process and significance; Ganong's potometer and limitations
  • Factors affecting rate of transpiration; experiments on transpiration
  • Mechanism of stomatal transpiration – potassium ion exchange theory
  • Adaptations to reduce transpiration; guttation and bleeding

(iii) Photosynthesis

  • Process and significance; balanced chemical equation
  • Internal structure of chloroplast; site of light and dark reactions
  • Opening and closing of stomata – potassium ion exchange theory
  • Light reaction (photochemical): activation of chlorophyll, photolysis of water, O₂ release, ATP formation, NADPH
  • Dark reaction (biosynthetic): CO₂ + hydrogen → glucose (no detailed equations)
  • Adaptations in plants for photosynthesis; experiments on photosynthesis
  • Carbon cycle – diagrammatic representation

(iv) Chemical Coordination in Plants

  • Plant growth regulators: Auxins, Gibberellins, Cytokinins, Abscisic acid, Ethylene
  • Tropic movements: Phototropism, Geotropism, Hydrotropism, Thigmotropism, Chemotropism
3. Human Anatomy and Physiology (Class X) ▼

(i) Circulatory System

  • Composition of blood – structure and functions of RBC, WBC, platelets
  • Tissue fluid and lymph; blood coagulation
  • Structure and working of the heart; main blood vessels entering/leaving heart, liver and kidney
  • Systole and diastole; double circulation; pulse and blood pressure
  • Blood vessels: artery, vein, capillary – structure and function
  • Lymphatic organs: spleen and tonsils
  • ABO blood group system, Rh factor; hepatic portal system
  • Efficiency of mammalian RBCs due to absence of certain organelles

(ii) Excretory System

  • Different excretory organs in the human body
  • External and internal structure of kidney; parts of urinary system
  • Blood vessels entering/leaving kidney; functions of urinary system parts
  • Structure and function of nephron; ultra-filtration (malpighian capsule); selective reabsorption; tubular secretion

(iii) Nervous System

  • Parts of a neuron; structure of the brain: Medulla Oblongata, Cerebrum, Cerebellum, Thalamus, Hypothalamus, Pons
  • White and gray matter in brain and spinal cord
  • Voluntary and involuntary actions; reflex arc; peripheral and autonomic nervous system
  • Natural and acquired reflex; differences
  • Eye – external and internal structure, functions, stereoscopic vision, adaptation, accommodation; defects: myopia, hypermetropia, presbyopia, astigmatism, cataract
  • Ear – structure and functions; course of perception of sound; role in balance

(iv) Endocrine System

  • Endocrine vs exocrine glands; location and shape of endocrine glands
  • Pancreas: insulin, glucagon; Thyroid: thyroxin; Adrenal: cortical hormones, adrenaline; Pituitary: growth hormone, tropic hormones, ADH, oxytocin
  • Hypo- and hypersecretion effects; feedback mechanism (TSH)

(v) Reproductive System

  • Male and female reproductive organs; male accessory glands; secondary sexual characters
  • Structure and functions of sperm and egg
  • Fertilization, implantation, placenta, gestation, parturition
  • Role of placenta in nutrition, respiration, excretion; endocrinal function
  • Foetal membranes and amniotic fluid; menstrual cycle outline
  • Sex hormones: Testosterone, Oestrogen, Progesterone
  • Identical and fraternal twins – meaning and differences
4. Population | 5. Human Evolution | 6. Pollution (Class X) ▼

4. Population

  • Population explosion; reasons; demography, density, birth/death/growth rate
  • Problems: unemployment, overexploitation, low per capita income, price rise, pollution, unequal wealth
  • Population control – Tubectomy and Vasectomy

5. Human Evolution

  • Human ancestors: Australopithecus, Homo habilis, Homo erectus, Neanderthals, Cro-Magnon, Homo sapiens sapiens – characteristics (bipedalism, cranial capacity, canine size, forehead, chin, body hair, height/posture)
  • Lamarck's theory – use and disuse of organs; vestigial organs
  • Darwin's natural selection – survival of the fittest; peppered moth adaptation

6. Pollution

  • Types and sources: Air (vehicular, industrial), Water (detergents, sewage, oil spills), Thermal, Soil (industrial/commercial waste, fertilizers), Biomedical, Radiation, Noise
  • Biodegradable and Non-biodegradable wastes – meaning and examples (DDT, plastics)
  • Effects: Greenhouse effect, Global warming, Acid rain, Ozone depletion
  • Control: unleaded petrol/CNG, social forestry, sewage treatment, ban on plastics, organic farming, Euro Bharat standard, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
Practical Work – Internal Assessment (Both Classes) ▼

Class IX – Plant Life

  • Onion peel under microscope; cross-pollinated flower (e.g. Hibiscus); germinating bean seed and maize grain

Class IX – Animal Life

  • Human cheek cell; identification of sugar, starch, protein, fat; specimens of Non-Chordata and Chordata; lung and skin structure; mechanism of breathing (Bell jar); waste disposal visits

Class X – Plant Life

  • Permanent slides of mitosis stages; diffusion (KMnO₄); osmosis (Thistle Funnel, potato); absorption; transpiration (Bell jar, cobalt chloride paper, Ganong's potometer); photosynthesis experiments (light, CO₂, chlorophyll, O₂ using hydrilla/elodea)

Class X – Animal Life

  • Structures of urinary system, heart, kidney, brain (models/charts); blood cells identification; ear and eye (models); endocrine glands identification

External Examiner: 10 Marks
Total: 20 Marks

Science (52) – Chemistry

Science Paper 2  |  ICSE 2027

Code 52
Class IX
Written Paper: 80 Marks | Duration: 2 Hours
Internal Assessment: 20 Marks
Note: All reactions studied with reference to reactants, products, conditions, observations, balanced equations and diagrams
1. The Language of Chemistry ▼
  • ›Symbols, valency (mono, di, tri, tetra valent), radicals (formulae and valencies), compound formulae
  • ›Chemical equations – definitions, balancing by hit and trial method
  • ›Relative Atomic and Molecular Masses (H or 1/12th of C-12 standard); calculation of molecular mass and percentage composition
2. Chemical Changes and Reactions ▼
  • ›Types: Direct combination, Decomposition, Displacement, Double decomposition (with equations)
  • ›Energy changes: Exothermic and endothermic reactions – evolution/absorption of heat, light, electricity
3. Water ▼

Water as a Universal Solvent

  • Solutions as mixtures; saturated solutions; effect of temperature on solubility

Hydrated and Anhydrous Substances

  • Water of crystallisation; anhydrous substances
  • Properties: Efflorescence, Deliquescence, Hygroscopy
  • Removal of hardness: by boiling, by washing soda

Drying Agents; Soft and Hard Water

  • Meaning and examples of drying and dehydrating agents
  • Soft vs hard water (action of soap); types and causes of hardness
4. Atomic Structure and Chemical Bonding (Class IX) ▼

(i) Structure of an Atom

  • Nucleus (protons, neutrons), electrons; mass number, atomic number
  • Electron distribution in orbits (2n² rule), Octet rule
  • Isotopes – definition and examples (H, C, Cl)

(ii) Electrovalent and Covalent Bonding

  • Electrovalent bond: definition, atomic orbit structure (NaCl, MgCl₂, CaO)
  • Covalent bond: definition, atomic orbit structure (H₂, Cl₂, O₂, N₂, HCl, H₂O, NH₃, CCl₄, CH₄)
5. The Periodic Table (Class IX) ▼
  • ›Döbereiner's Triads, Newland's law of Octaves, Mendeleev's periodic law
  • ›Atomic number and Modern Periodic Law; Modern Periodic Table (Groups 1–18, Periods 1–7)
  • ›Alkali metals (Grp 1), Alkaline Earth metals (Grp 2), Halogens (Grp 17), Zero Group (Grp 18)
6. Study of Hydrogen ▼

(i) Hydrogen from Water, Acids, Alkalis

  • Action of cold water on Na, K, Ca; hot water on Mg; steam on Al, Zn, Fe, C (reversibility with iron)
  • Displacement from dilute H₂SO₄ / HCl on Mg, Al, Zn, Fe; reasons for not using dilute HNO₃
  • Action of NaOH / KOH on Al, Zn, Pb

(ii–iv) Preparation, Manufacture, Oxidation-Reduction

  • Laboratory preparation using zinc; impurities and their removal
  • Bosch process – main reactions, conditions, separation of CO₂ and CO
  • Oxidation and reduction – in terms of addition/removal of oxygen/hydrogen
7. Study of Gas Laws ▼
  • ›Behaviour of gases under temperature/pressure; molecular motion explanation
  • ›Boyle's Law and Charles' Law – statement, mathematical form, simple calculations
  • ›Absolute zero; Kelvin scale; gas equation P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂
  • ›Celsius ↔ Kelvin conversion; Standard Temperature and Pressure
8. Atmospheric Pollution (Class IX) ▼
  • ›Acid rain – composition, causes (sulphur in fossil fuels, oxides of N from furnaces/engines), impact on soil and water
  • ›Global warming – greenhouse gases (H₂O vapour, CO₂, CH₄, oxides of N) – sources and reduction methods
  • ›Ozone depletion – ozone formation, function in atmosphere, chemicals responsible (names only; reactions not required)
Class X
Written Paper: 80 Marks | Duration: 2 Hours
Internal Assessment: 20 Marks
1. Periodic Properties (Class X) ▼
  • ›Definitions and trends in groups and periods: atomic size, metallic character, non-metallic character, ionisation potential, electron affinity, electronegativity
  • ›Modern periodic table up to period 4 (up to Calcium); periodicity based on nuclear charge and shells
  • ›Special reference to alkali metals and halogen groups
2. Chemical Bonding (Class X) ▼

(a) Electrovalent

  • Electron dot structure: NaCl, MgCl₂, CaO
  • Characteristic properties: state, melting/boiling points, conductivity, dissociation in solution and molten state (linked to electrolysis)

(b) Covalent

  • Electron dot structure; non-polar (H₂, Cl₂, O₂, N₂, CCl₄, CH₄) and polar (HCl, NH₃, H₂O) compounds
  • Characteristic properties; comparison of electrovalent and covalent compounds

(c) Coordinate Bond

  • Definition; lone pair effect of oxygen (water) and nitrogen (ammonia) forming H₃O⁺, OH⁻, NH₄⁺ (with electron dot diagrams)
3. Acids, Bases and Salts (Class X) ▼
  • ›Simple definitions; characteristic properties; ions in mineral acids, alkalis, salts
  • ›Acid-base indicators (litmus, methyl orange, phenolphthalein); pH paper; pH scale
  • ›Types of salts: normal, acid, basic, complex
  • ›Action of dilute acids on salts: hydrogen carbonates, carbonates, sulphites, sulphides
  • ›Preparation of normal salts: direct combination, displacement, precipitation, neutralization, action on carbonates and bicarbonates
4. Analytical Chemistry ▼
  • ›Action of NaOH and NH₄OH on solutions of Fe, Cu, Zn, Pb salts – colour and hydroxide precipitates
  • ›Special action of NH₄OH on copper salts; NaOH on ammonium salts
  • ›Action of alkalis (NaOH, KOH) on Al, Zn, Pb – their oxides and hydroxides (amphoteric nature)
5. Mole Concept and Stoichiometry ▼
  • ›Gay Lussac's Law of Combining Volumes; Avogadro's Law and molar volume (22.4 L at STP)
  • ›Atomicity of H₂, O₂, N₂, Cl₂; Vapour density – relation to relative molecular mass (M = 2×VD)
  • ›Empirical and molecular formula from percentage composition and masses
  • ›Mole and its relation to mass, volume, Avogadro's number; calculations based on chemical equations
6. Electrolysis ▼
  • ›Electrolytes and non-electrolytes; strong and weak electrolytes
  • ›Electrolysis: terms – electrolyte, electrode, anode, cathode, anion, cation, oxidation, reduction (loss/gain of electrons)
  • ›Migration of ions; selective discharge theory; electrolysis of: molten PbBr₂, acidified water (Pt), aqueous CuSO₄ (Cu and Pt electrodes)
  • ›Applications: electroplating (Ni, Ag – electrolytes and electrodes); electro-refining of copper
7. Metallurgy ▼
  • ›Mineral and ore; common ores of iron, aluminium, zinc
  • ›Stages: Pulverization/dressing (hydraulic, magnetic, froth flotation); roasting and calcination; reduction of metallic oxides (by H₂, C, CO); active metals by electrolysis
  • ›Extraction of aluminium: Baeyer's process (chemical purification); Hall-Heroult process (electrolytic extraction – cell structure, electrode reactions)
  • ›Alloy and Amalgam: composition and uses of stainless steel, magnalium, duralumin, brass, bronze, fuse metal/solder
8. Study of Compounds – HCl, Ammonia, HNO₃, H₂SO₄ ▼

A. Hydrogen Chloride

  • Preparation from NaCl; density, solubility (fountain experiment); reaction with ammonia; acidic properties; aqua regia; precipitation reactions with AgNO₃ and Pb(NO₃)₂; tests

B. Ammonia

  • Lab preparation from NH₄Cl; from nitrides (Mg₃N₂, AlN) and ammonium salts
  • Haber's process – manufacture; density and solubility (fountain experiment)
  • Reactions: burning in O₂; catalytic oxidation; with HCl; with hot CuO, PbO₂, Cl₂; aqueous solution reactions with acids, Fe, Cu, Pb, Zn salts
  • Uses: fertilizers, explosives, HNO₃, refrigerant; tests

C. Nitric Acid

  • Lab preparation from KNO₃ or NaNO₃; Ostwald's process (equations with conditions)
  • As oxidizing agent: reactions with Cu, C, S; acidic properties; tests

D. Sulphuric Acid

  • Contact process (equations with conditions)
  • Dilute: reactions with metals, metal oxides, hydroxides, carbonates, sulphites, sulphides
  • Concentrated: oxidizing agent (C, S); dehydrating agent (sugar, CuSO₄ crystals); non-volatile nature; dibasic acid; tests
9. Organic Chemistry ▼

(i–iv) Introduction, Structure, Isomerism, Nomenclature

  • Unique nature of carbon: tetravalency, catenation
  • Single, double, triple bonds; straight chain, branched chain, cyclic (benzene structure)
  • Structural formulae for alkanes, alkenes, alkynes up to 5 carbon atoms; isomerism (chain, position)
  • Homologous series; IUPAC and trivial names for functional groups (halides, double/triple bond, alcohol, aldehyde, carboxylic, ketone, ether, ester)

(v) Hydrocarbons

  • Alkanes: general formula; methane (greenhouse gas) and ethane – preparation from sodium ethanoate, sodium propanoate, iodomethane, bromoethane; complete and incomplete combustion; reaction with Cl₂ (substitution)
  • Alkenes (double bond); ethene – preparation by dehydrohalogenation and dehydration; addition reactions with H₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂
  • Alkynes (triple bond); ethyne – preparation from CaC₂ and 1,2-dibromoethane; addition reactions
  • Uses of methane, ethane, ethene, ethyne

(vi) Ethanol

  • Preparation by hydrolysis of alkyl halide
  • Physical and chemical properties: combustion, action with Na, ester formation, dehydration
  • Denatured/methylated spirit; uses

(vii) Acetic Acid

  • Structure; physical properties (odour, glacial acetic acid); chemical properties (litmus, alkalis, esterification); uses

Science (52) – Physics

Science Paper 1  |  ICSE 2027

Code 52
Class IX
Written Paper: 80 Marks | Duration: 2 Hours
Internal Assessment: 20 Marks
SI units only unless otherwise specified
1. Measurements and Experimentation ▼
  • ›SI units with correct symbols; fps and cgs systems
  • ›Vernier callipers and micrometer screw gauge (least count, zero error – no numerical problems)
  • ›Simple pendulum: time period, frequency, l vs T² graph, slope, formula T = 2π√(l/g) (no derivation); numerical problems
2. Motion in One Dimension ▼
  • ›Scalar and vector quantities; distance, displacement, speed, velocity, acceleration, retardation
  • ›Distance-time and velocity-time graphs; meaning of slope
  • ›Equations of uniformly accelerated motion (derived): v=u+at; S=ut+½at²; S=½(u+v)t; v²=u²+2aS (Snth not included)
3. Laws of Motion ▼
  • ›Contact forces (frictional, normal, tension, collision) and non-contact forces (gravitational, electric, magnetic); cgs and SI units
  • ›Newton's 1st Law – inertia, mass and force; examples of inertia
  • ›Newton's 2nd Law – linear momentum; F = Δp/Δt = ma; weight and mass
  • ›Newton's 3rd Law – action-reaction pairs (qualitative)
  • ›Gravitation: Universal Law (statement and equation); gravity; free fall; gravitational units of force (problems on variation excluded)
4. Fluids ▼
  • ›Thrust and pressure; p = hρg; Pascal's law; atmospheric pressure; variation with altitude
  • ›Buoyancy; upthrust; Archimedes' principle; Relative Density; floatation (ρ₁/ρ₂ = V₂/V₁); applications (ship, submarine, iceberg, balloon)
5. Heat and Energy ▼
  • ›Heat as energy; SI unit joule; 1 cal = 4.186 J
  • ›Anomalous expansion of water (0–10°C); Hope's experiment; consequences
  • ›Energy flow – laws of thermodynamics (energy conservation and efficiency)
  • ›Energy sources: solar, wind, water, nuclear; renewable vs non-renewable; biogas, tidal, run-of-river; economic viability
  • ›Global warming and Greenhouse effect – meaning, causes, impact, projections; energy degradation
6. Light (Class IX) ▼
  • ›Reflection: laws (experimental verification); images in parallel and perpendicular plane mirrors; uses of plane mirrors
  • ›Spherical mirrors (concave and convex): centre and radius of curvature, pole, principal axis, focus, focal length; ray diagrams for all positions; f = R/2; sign convention; mirror formula (no derivation); uses (scale drawing not required)
7. Sound (Class IX)  &  8. Electricity and Magnetism (Class IX) ▼

7. Sound

  • Nature of sound waves; medium requirement; propagation and speed in different media; V = fλ; factors affecting speed; thunder and lightning
  • Infrasonic, sonic, ultrasonic frequencies – applications; difference between ultrasonic and supersonic

8. Electricity and Magnetism

  • Simple electric circuit; current (rate of flow of charge); potential difference V = W/q; insulators and conductors; direction of current (electron flow and conventional)
  • Induced magnetism; magnetic field of earth; neutral points; lines of magnetic field; compass; bar magnet along magnetic N–S
  • Electromagnet and its uses
Class X
Written Paper: 80 Marks | Duration: 2 Hours
Internal Assessment: 20 Marks
1. Force, Work, Power and Energy (Class X) ▼
  • ›Turning forces; moment/torque; equilibrium (translational and rotational); principle of moments; centre of gravity
  • ›Uniform circular motion; centrifugal vs centripetal force
  • ›Work W = FS cosθ; W = mgh; energy types; units (erg, cal, kWh, eV); Power P = W/t; kW, MW, GW, horsepower
  • ›Potential energy U = mgh (derived); kinetic energy K = ½mv² (derived); translational, rotational, vibrational KE
  • ›Machines: effort, load, MA = L/E, VR, efficiency η; MA < VR; levers (1st, 2nd, 3rd class, examples in human body); pulley systems (single fixed, single movable, block and tackle)
  • ›Principle of conservation of energy; U + K = constant (freely falling body); simple pendulum application
2. Light (Class X) ▼
  • ›Refraction: laws; effect on speed, wavelength, frequency; refractive index μ = c/V; V = fλ; glass block; lateral displacement; real and apparent depth; apparent bending of sticks
  • ›Total internal reflection: critical angle μ = 1/sin C; right-angle prism (δ = 90° and 180°); comparison with plane mirror
  • ›Lenses: converging and diverging; ray diagrams for all positions; sign convention; lens formula (no derivation); power; magnifying glass (ray diagram only)
  • ›Prism and dispersion; electromagnetic spectrum (names in order of wavelength); infrared and ultraviolet; blue colour of sky (scattering)
3. Sound (Class X) | 4. Electricity & Magnetism (Class X) | 5. Heat | 6. Modern Physics ▼

3. Sound (Class X)

  • Echoes: production, condition, numerical problems; uses (bats, dolphins, SONAR)
  • Natural, damped, forced vibrations; resonance
  • Loudness, intensity, pitch, quality; sound level in dB; noise pollution

4. Electricity and Magnetism (Class X)

  • Ohm's Law (V = IR); emf, pd, resistance, charge; ohmic and non-ohmic; specific resistance; superconductors; resistances in series and parallel (derived)
  • Electrical energy W = QV = VIt = I²Rt = V²t/R; power P = VI = I²R = V²/R; units; kWh; household consumption
  • House wiring (ring system); live/neutral/earth (3 wires); fuse/MCB; two-way switch; staircase wiring; earthing; colour coding; 3-pin plug and socket; safety precautions
  • Magnetic effect of current: Oersted's experiment; right-hand thumb rule; field due to a loop; electromagnets; Fleming's Left Hand Rule; DC motor (sketch and description)
  • Electromagnetic induction; Fleming's Right Hand Rule; AC generator; AC over DC advantages; transformer types, characteristics, uses

5. Heat (Class X)

  • Calorimetry: specific heat capacity C = Q/(mΔT); thermal capacity C' = Q/ΔT; method of mixtures; consequences of high specific heat of water
  • Latent heat; specific latent heat of fusion; heating curve; change of state

6. Modern Physics

  • Radioactivity: nuclear structure; atomic number Z, mass number A; α, β, γ – nature, properties, changes in nucleus; one example each of α and β decay
  • Radioactive isotopes – uses; harmful effects; safety precautions; background radiation; X-rays; nuclear energy – safe disposal
  • Nuclear fission and fusion – basic introduction and equations

1. Pure Arithmetic – Rational & Irrational Numbers ▼
  • ›Rational and irrational numbers as real numbers; place in number system
  • ›Surds; rationalization of denominators; simplifying expressions
  • ›Representation on number line
  • ›Proofs of irrationality of √2, √3, √5
2. Commercial Mathematics – Compound Interest ▼
  • ›CI as repeated SI computation; A = P(1 + r/100)ⁿ; CI = A − P
  • ›Interest compounded half-yearly; finding A, P, r, n, CI, SI given other quantities
  • ›Difference between CI and SI; rate of growth and depreciation
3. Algebra (Class IX) ▼

(i) Expansions

  • (a ± b)², (a ± b)³, (x ± a)(x ± b), (a ± b ± c)²

(ii) Factorisation

  • a² − b², a³ ± b³, ax² + bx + c (splitting middle term)

(iii–v) Equations, Indices, Logarithms

  • Simultaneous linear equations in two variables: elimination, substitution, cross multiplication
  • Indices: positive, fractional, negative, zero indices; laws of exponents
  • Logarithms: log form vs exponential form; laws of logarithms; expansion of expressions using logs
4. Geometry (Class IX) ▼

(i) Triangles

  • Congruency: SSS, SAS, AAS, RHS
  • Angles opposite equal sides (and converse); inequalities in triangles; triangle inequality
  • Mid-Point Theorem (proof and applications); Equal Intercept Theorem
  • Pythagoras Theorem – area-based proof and applications (and converse)

(ii) Rectilinear Figures

  • Parallelogram theorems (opposite sides, angles, diagonals bisect each other); rhombus, rectangle, square properties
  • Constructions: quadrilaterals (including parallelograms, rhombus) and regular hexagon
  • Area theorems: parallelograms on same base/between same parallels; triangle as half parallelogram; equal-base equal-area

(iii) Circle

  • Chord properties: perpendicular from centre bisects chord; equal chords equidistant from centre; one circle through three non-collinear points
  • Arc and chord properties: equal arcs subtend equal angles (and converse)
5. Statistics | 6. Mensuration | 7. Trigonometry | 8. Co-ordinate Geometry (Class IX) ▼

5. Statistics (Class IX)

  • Raw, arrayed, grouped data; tally-marks; discrete and continuous variables
  • Mean and median of ungrouped data
  • Class intervals, boundaries, frequency tables; grouped frequency distributions; frequency polygon

6. Mensuration (Class IX)

  • Area and perimeter of triangle (including Heron's formula) and all types of quadrilaterals
  • Circle: area and circumference; inner and outer area problems
  • 3D solids: cube and cuboid – surface area, volume, cost, open/closed

7. Trigonometry (Class IX)

  • Trigonometric ratios: sin, cos, tan and their reciprocals
  • Standard angles: 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°; evaluation of expressions
  • Simple 2D problems with one right-angled triangle
  • Complementary angles: sin A = cos(90−A), tan A = cot(90−A), etc.

8. Co-ordinate Geometry (Class IX)

  • Cartesian system; ordered pairs; plotting points; solving simultaneous equations graphically
  • Distance formula
Class X

Internal Assessment: 20 Marks
1. Commercial Mathematics (Class X) ▼
  • ›GST: computation including discounts, list price, profit/loss, SGST, CGST; inverse cases
  • ›Banking – Recurring Deposit: I = P·n(n+1)/(2×12) × r/100; MV = P×n + I

2. Algebra (Class X) ▼

(i–iii) Inequations, Quadratic Equations, Ratio and Proportion

  • Linear inequations in one unknown (N, W, Z, R); algebraic solution; number line representation
  • Quadratic equations: nature of roots (discriminant b²−4ac); solving by factorisation and formula; word problems
  • Proportion: continued proportion, mean proportion; componendo, dividendo, alternendo, invertendo; direct applications

(iv–vii) Polynomials, Matrices, AP/GP, Co-ordinate Geometry

  • Factor Theorem, Remainder Theorem; factorising polynomial up to degree 3
  • Matrices: order; row/column matrices; null and identity matrices; compatibility for addition and multiplication; 2×2 matrix addition, subtraction, multiplication by scalar and matrix

3. Geometry (Class X) ▼

(a) Similarity

  • Similarity as size transformation; conditions SSS, SAS, AA; Basic Proportionality Theorem
  • Areas of similar triangles ∝ squares of corresponding sides; maps and models applications

(b) Loci

  • Fixed distance from point (circle); equidistant from two intersecting lines (angle bisector); equidistant from two points (perpendicular bisector)

(c) Circles

  • Angle in a semicircle; angles in same segment; arc subtends double angle at centre
  • Cyclic quadrilateral: opposite angles supplementary; exterior angle = opposite interior angle
